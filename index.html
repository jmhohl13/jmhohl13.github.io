About Me

Currently I am a sophomore Software Engineering and Computer Science major at Rose-Hulman. I hail from Middletown, Maryland, which is a suburb of Frederick. I grew up playing soccer all my life and being involved in the Boy Scouts of America. I became interested in computer science after taking a beginning level coding course in high school. I chose to come to Rose-Hulman after attending Operation Catapult, a summer program for kids interested in engineering. Here at school I stay busy with classwork, intramurals, and personal project/online classes. I am also the Treasurer of the Pi Kappa Alpha Fraternity, an organization here of over 110 members.

For more information about me check out my resume or shoot me an email!

Experience

This past summer I worked at hc1.com, a medical cloud company. I learned an immeasurable amount, and most of it I could not have learned in classroom. Perhaps the most important thing I learned, I learned from my supervisor. He told me: "You don't go to college to learn everything you need to know. You go to learn how to learn. No matter where you end up you will always be learning, college just teaches you how to do that".

My only hope for next summer is to get a job where I can learn and be challenged as much as I was in this past one
